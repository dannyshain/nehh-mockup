Did You Know?

Small maintenance, big consequences

Most home hazards don't announce themselves. Here's why the little jobs on our list matter more than they look.

2,900 fires/year

Clogged dryer vents cause an estimated 2,900 home fires every year in the U.S.

Lint is highly flammable, and it builds up out of sight inside the vent line. If your dryer takes two cycles to dry a load, that's the warning sign.

Book a dryer vent cleaning → Source: U.S. Fire Administration / FEMA report (PDF)
1 in 4 seniors falls each year

One in four adults 65+ falls every year — and most falls at home happen in the bathroom.

Properly installed grab bars, railings, and better lighting are among the most effective ways to prevent them. A one-hour visit can make the highest-risk rooms dramatically safer.

Book a home safety assessment → Source: CDC & CDC MMWR bathroom-injury data
53 minutes

Every 53 minutes, a child is treated in a U.S. emergency room for a furniture or TV tip-over injury.

Nearly 80% of tip-over deaths involve children 5 and under — and anchoring furniture to the wall takes minutes. It's the single highest-impact babyproofing step there is.

Book babyproofing → Source: U.S. CPSC (2024)
$15,400 average claim

The average water-damage or frozen-pipe insurance claim runs about $15,400 — and 1 in 60 insured homes files one every year.

Most of that damage starts small: a drip, a freeze, a failed hose. In an empty house, small runs for weeks. Regular checks are the difference.

Set up home watch → Source: Insurance Information Institute
