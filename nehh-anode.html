Home Maintenance

Water Heater Anode Check / Replace — $149

Inside every tank water heater hangs a metal rod with one job: corrode. The anode rod is deliberately made of metal that rusts more eagerly than steel, so the water attacks it instead of the tank walls. It's called a sacrificial anode because that's the deal — the rod dies so the tank doesn't.

Once the rod is used up, the tank itself starts corroding, and from that point the clock is running. Given that water heating is the second-largest energy expense in most homes — about 18% of energy use, per the Department of Energy — a healthy, efficient tank is worth protecting. A $30 rod checked every few years can add years to a tank's life; a new water heater costs well over a thousand dollars installed.

The Service

What we actually do

We shut the heater down safely, pull the anode rod, and inspect it. If it still has sacrificial metal left, it goes back in and we tell you when to check again. If it's spent — a bare steel wire, or coated in calcium — we install a new one matched to your tank and water. Either way, you'll see the rod yourself and know exactly where your tank stands.

$149 flat-rate labor, rod purchased by you or through NEHH. Pairs perfectly with a water heater flush ($99) — same shutdown, one visit, and the tank gets both of its life-extending services at once.
